سارة المختار

I’m Sarah Almukhtar, a designer and journalist formerly at The New York Times. I use tools like HTML, CSS, Javascript, Illustrator, and Photoshop to design and code interactive stories. Here are my bylines and my resume.

American DowntownsI worked with editors to bring together ten essays on American downtowns. Navigation design, photo layout, and mapping were key elements.
MENA IdentityWe used illustration and color to reflect the diversity of responses to a survey. I designed the layout and hierarchy and worked with the illustrator on direction and transitions.
One Million Deaths From CovidMy primary role was to design a visual system that could convey the magnitude of loss. I collaborated with data journalists and editors to structure the story and transition between graphics.
Extreme HeatWe used color and labels to try to help the user understand the heat the subjects felt. I contributed to the design of the chapter hierarchy, typography, and visual breaks.
The New York City MixtapeWe wanted this multimedia piece to feel playful. Color and transition let the user move in and out of different musical scenes.
36 Hours RedesignI was responsible for turning this long-standing column into a much more visual experience while maintaining its signature itinerary. We emphasized photography, short lists, and multiple navigation points to improve the user experience. I also worked with and trained several travel editors to keep the production process accessible to them.
The New SkatersI used typography, color, and layering to showcase as many portraits as possible and reflect the boldness of women skaters who are creating new communities in New York City.
Women in AfghanistanI collaborated with photo editors to structure the story and design the presentation of a moving collection of portraits taken across Afghanistan.
Parsing American StyleThe goal was to create a digital lookbook of everyday American style. We collaborated with the photo editor to find similarities between photos and create a visual flow.
Love/Hate Valentine’s Day MoviesWe used illustration, color, and animation throughout to create interactive, humorous movie lists.
Year in Graphics 2020For this yearly review of work published by the Graphics and Digital Design desks, we designed a chronological gallery incorporating digital and print pieces and the voices of team members.
What It’s Like to Live With Parkinson’sWe worked with the illustrator to create interactive visuals that evoke the disorientation caused by a Parkinson’s diagnosis.
